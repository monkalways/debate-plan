ARGUMENT: captive breeding is the last line against extinction
Side: Opp        Motion type: Abolition
Burden it serves: the irreversibility limb — a ban forecloses the only backstop for species with no viable wild population left.

CLAIM: Zoos breed, protect, and reintroduce species that would otherwise be gone for good.

CHAIN:
  L1  Species extinct/near-extinct in the wild → captive breeding is the remaining refuge
      Warrant: when wild numbers collapse, a managed captive population is the only breeding stock left.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#Przewalski's horse]] — declared extinct in the wild (1960s), ~12 left in zoos ⚠️(verify figures)
  L2  Captive breeding → numbers recover → reintroduction
      Warrant: protected breeding rebuilds a population releasable when ready.
      Evidence: Przewalski's horse now ~2,000, with some returned to the wild ⚠️(verify)
  L3  Ban → the refuge and the genetic stock are destroyed
      Warrant: extinction is absolute — once the last individual and its genome are gone, no future action recovers it.

  Weighing: irreversibility — extinction can't be undone; captive discomfort is reversible. Warranted to the floor: "when the final horse is gone, its entire genetic print is gone from Earth."
