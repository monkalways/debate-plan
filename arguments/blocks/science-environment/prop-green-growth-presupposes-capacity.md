ARGUMENT: "green growth / don't choose" fails — the green path presupposes the very capacity growth builds  🔁
Side: Prop        Motion type: Comparative  (growth-vs-environment; deploys as a rebuttal shield)
Burden it serves: defeats Opp's "false dichotomy — you don't have to choose; renewables let poor nations leapfrog" line

CLAIM: "Green growth" isn't a free third option for a developing country, because the renewable transition is capital-intensive up front — and a state too poor to enforce a logging ban is far too poor to fund a grid of solar, wind and dams. You need the capacity first; growth is how you get it.

CHAIN:
  L1  Opp offers "green growth / leapfrog to renewables" as a no-trade-off path
      Warrant: (their claim) you can grow AND protect via clean tech, so Prop's "choose growth" is a false choice.
  L2  the green path is heavily capital-intensive in the short run
      Warrant: solar farms, wind turbines, hydro dams and the grid to carry them are huge upfront investments; a subsistence economy can't front that cost, so "leapfrogging" stalls for want of capital.
  L3  no capital → the transition is delayed by decades → development AND the environment both stall
      Warrant: the poor state gets neither growth nor a clean grid — it's stuck. Only prioritising growth first builds the capital that later funds both the clean transition and enforcement.

IMPACT: the "don't choose" option is a mirage for a capacity-poor state — insisting on it locks in decades of stalled development and an unprotected environment. Growth-first is the only *sequence* that actually reaches a protected environment.
  Weighing: probability / timeframe — green-growth-first assumes a capital base that doesn't exist yet; growth-first is the only path that plausibly delivers both, in order.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: renewables are cheap now — poor nations can leapfrog dirty industry.  → I say: panels are cheaper, but the grid, storage and installation are still huge upfront capital a subsistence state can't front; cheap per-panel ≠ affordable at national scale without a tax base first.
  They say: waiting for growth locks in irreversible damage.  → I say: poverty degrades the environment NOW (desperate people clear forests, over-fish), and an unfunded green plan doesn't stop it — you protect nothing you can't pay to enforce. [[prop-growth-buys-enforcement-capacity]]
