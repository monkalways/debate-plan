ARGUMENT: medical research depends on animals → banning costs lives
Side: Opp        Motion type: Abolition
Burden it serves: the practical / consequentialist limb — the ban has a body count.

CLAIM: Banning animal research strips out the drug pipeline that keeps millions alive.

CHAIN:
  L1  Ban → removes animals from the research pipeline
      Warrant: a large share of research and mandatory safety testing runs through animals.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#NIH animal-research funding (PETA)]] — PETA: ~47% of NIH-funded research involves animals; NIH ~$42B R&D (2020)
  L2  No pipeline → core drugs can't be developed / safety-passed
      Warrant: landmark drugs required animal testing to exist and to clear safety.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#Insulin & dogs]] (isolated in dogs, 1921); [[examples-bank#mRNA COVID-19 vaccines]] (tested in mice/macaques before rollout)
  L3  No drugs → mass preventable death
      Warrant: removing the pipeline denies future treatments to hundreds of millions.

IMPACT: Millions of preventable human deaths.
  Weighing: scope — millions of human lives vs. the welfare of test animals. (⚠️ near-duplicates the human-life block's magnitude weigh — differentiate the axes.)

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: you're citing PETA, an anti-testing group.  → I say: exactly — even the movement's own body admits ~47% of NIH research depends on animals; the scale isn't my number, it's theirs. (deliberate opponent-turn)
  They say: alternatives can do this now.  → I say: (alternatives block) they supplement, not replace — regulators still mandate whole-animal safety data.
