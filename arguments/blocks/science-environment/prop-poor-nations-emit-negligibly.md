ARGUMENT: a poor nation greening itself barely moves the global total
Side: Prop        Motion type: Comparative
Burden it serves: shows the environmental COST of prioritising growth in a developing country is small — its emissions are a rounding error.

CLAIM: Forcing a developing country to sacrifice growth for emissions cuts is futile and unjust, because its emissions are negligible in the global total.

CHAIN:
  L1  Poor country prioritises environment over growth → it slows its own economy
      Warrant: green options are costlier and slower; diverting from growth has a real domestic price.
      Evidence: —
  L2  Its emissions cut → negligible change to the global total
      Warrant: it emits a tiny share; even zeroing it out barely moves the aggregate that drives warming.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#Emissions inequality (giant vs. poor nation)]] — US ~an eighth of global CO2 vs a poor nation well under 1%
  L3  Negligible global effect + real domestic cost → net harm
      Warrant: the country pays full price (lost growth) for near-zero climate benefit; leverage sits with the giants (US, China).

IMPACT: the poorest bear growth costs for no planetary gain — inefficient and unjust.
  Weighing: scope / stakeholder — the burden falls on those least responsible and least able to bear it.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: everyone must do their part / lead by example.  → I say: example-setting from a <1% emitter has near-zero effect on the ~13% emitters; pressure the giants, don't hobble the poor.
  They say: developing emissions are the fastest-growing.  → I say: total and per-capita remain tiny; the marginal ton is cheaper to cut in rich high-emitters, and growth now buys the wealth to green later.
