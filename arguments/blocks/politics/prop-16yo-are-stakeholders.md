ARGUMENT: enfranchising the affected forces politicians to serve them
Side: Prop        Motion type: Policy
Burden it serves: proves lowering the voting age produces materially better policy for 16–17s (not just a rights nicety)

CLAIM: Giving 16-year-olds the vote makes politicians actually represent their interests, because a group that can't vote is invisible to vote-seekers.

CHAIN:
  L1  16-year-olds can't vote → politicians ignore their interests
      Warrant: a politician's first goal is re-election; a group that can't help you win is not worth serving, so laws on education, child welfare and digital privacy are drafted without them in mind.
      Evidence: — (structural incentive, no stat needed)
  L2  Enfranchise 16-year-olds → the electoral calculation flips
      Warrant: the student is now a voter; the politician who ignores schools is now the politician losing votes, so platforms shift toward the people inside those systems.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#Austria — voting age 16]] — 16–17s turned out well and their choices were "just as considered" as older voters
  L3  Platforms shift toward the affected → policy improves for them
      Warrant: those who live inside the education/child-welfare system understand it in a way adult drafters don't, so representation improves policy quality, not just fairness.

IMPACT: better-targeted policy for ~2 birth-years of citizens directly governed by laws they had no say in — a democratic-legitimacy and welfare gain.
  Weighing: stakeholder-priority — "a less-informed vote still represents a real interest; a silenced interest represents nothing at all." Beats Opp's quality concern because the counterfactual isn't a better vote, it's no voice.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: less-informed 16-year-olds lower election quality.  → I say: quality of an unheard interest is zero; a slightly-less-informed vote that represents a real stake beats silence — and Austria shows their choices were as considered anyway.
  They say: 16yos just vote as parents/teachers tell them — the vote isn't independent (Yoodli follow-up).  → I say: influence isn't unique to teens (adults are swayed by family/party/media); the secret ballot protects their real choice, and Austria shows they diverge from parents.
