ARGUMENT: "forced turnout = junk votes" fails — junk exists in both worlds and it's a small honest cost  🔁
Side: Prop        Motion type: Policy  (rebuttal shield)
Burden it serves: neutralizes the "compulsory voting just adds uninformed / donkey / informal ballots" objection

CLAIM: The "you'll just get uninformed votes" objection doesn't sink compulsory voting, because uninformed voting exists in both worlds and the extra invalid ballots are a small, honest cost against a far larger legitimacy gain.

CHAIN:
  L1  objection: forced turnout → more uninformed / donkey / informal votes
      Warrant: (their claim) people dragged to the poll tick randomly, degrading the result.
  L2  rebuttal (a): uninformed voting already exists under voluntary systems → it's a wash, not a new harm
      Warrant: plenty of voluntary voters vote on vibes / party loyalty; compulsion doesn't *create* low-information voting, it just changes the count.
  L3  rebuttal (b): required to show up → most inform themselves + invalid ballots stay small
      Warrant: being required to turn up raises voting's salience, so people read platforms rather than waste the trip; and where it's compulsory, only ~5% of ballots come back informal — 19 in 20 count.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#Australia — compulsory voting]] — informal rate ~5%

IMPACT: the objection is defused — the quality cost is real but small and *symmetric*; the legitimacy and equal-voice gains dwarf it.
  Weighing: magnitude — ~5% invalid ballots vs. the legitimacy of the whole electorate + a voice for the previously-silent majority of non-voters.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: 5%+ informal ballots proves compulsion produces junk.  → I say: name it correctly — that's the *informal* (spoiled) rate; actual "donkey votes" (numbered 1-2-3 straight down the page) are only ~1–2%. 95% valid is the honest, small price of near-universal turnout.
  They say: an uninformed vote is worse than no vote.  → I say: an unheard interest counts as zero; a slightly-less-informed vote that represents a real stake beats silence. (cross-link [[prop-16yo-are-stakeholders]])
