ARGUMENT: "compulsory voting won't change the result" is false — abstention isn't ideologically neutral  🔁
Side: Prop        Motion type: Policy  (rebuttal shield)
Burden it serves: defeats the "it's a wash — every non-voting Liberal = a non-voting Conservative" objection

CLAIM: Forcing turnout does change outcomes, because the people who abstain aren't a random, neutral slice — they lean systematically, so compelling them moves the median voter.

CHAIN:
  L1  objection: non-voters are spread evenly across parties → mandating them cancels out (a wash)
      Warrant: (their claim) if abstention is random, adding it changes nothing.
  L2  rebuttal: abstention correlates with ideology & circumstance, not randomly
      Warrant: anti-government / low-trust voters and the poor / young abstain more; someone who wants *less* government interaction is less motivated to show up than someone who wants government to act.
      Evidence: — (illustrate tightly: "Gerald" distrusts government → less likely to vote; "John" wants government action → more likely — one line each)
  L3  non-random abstention → compulsion adds a *skewed* bloc → shifts the median voter, not just the count
      Warrant: adding the systematically-absent changes which platform wins, so the compulsory result genuinely differs from the voluntary one.

IMPACT: the objection collapses — compulsory voting isn't cosmetic; it changes who wins, and therefore whose interests govern.
  Weighing: probability — the wash claim *needs* abstention to be perfectly random (implausible); systematic skew is the far likelier world.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: you can't prove which way non-voters lean.  → I say: I don't need the exact split — I only need abstention to be *non-random*, and barriers + distrust demonstrably fall harder on identifiable groups (poor, young, anti-government), so it can't be a clean wash.
  They say: then compulsory voting is just your side rigging turnout.  → I say: no — it's counting *everyone*; if a fuller electorate favours one side, that's the democratic result the voluntary system was suppressing.
