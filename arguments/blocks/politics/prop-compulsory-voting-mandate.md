ARGUMENT: universal turnout gives a government a mandate everyone accepts
Side: Prop        Motion type: Policy
Burden it serves: proves compulsory voting improves democratic *legitimacy* (not merely the turnout number)

CLAIM: Compulsory voting gives a government a real mandate, because a result everyone helped decide is a result everyone has a reason to accept.

CHAIN:
  L1  voluntary voting → a government can win on a minority of eligible adults
      Warrant: when turnout is ~two-thirds, a winning coalition can rest on ~30% of eligible voters; the majority never actively endorsed it.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#Canada — 2025 federal turnout]] — 69% turnout means ~31% never voted
  L2  minority mandate → the unrepresented distrust and disengage
      Warrant: people who feel the outcome doesn't reflect them lose faith in the result and withdraw further — a legitimacy doom-loop.
  L3  compulsory voting → the whole demos decides → broad acceptance
      Warrant: when everyone is counted the winner reflects the actual population, so even losers have reason to accept a result that was fully and fairly decided → durable, legitimate government.

IMPACT: a stable, widely-accepted government; less polarization and more trust in the system as a whole.
  Weighing: magnitude — the legitimacy of the entire democracy outweighs a small fraction of low-quality ballots (see [[prop-informal-votes-wash-both-worlds]]).

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: forcing turnout just produces uninformed/donkey votes that pollute the mandate.  → I say: compulsion is only to show up and be *counted* — you can spoil the ballot; most, once required, inform themselves rather than waste the trip, and informal ballots run ~5%, a small honest cost. [[prop-informal-votes-wash-both-worlds]]
  They say: forcing a vote violates freedom — abstention is itself political speech.  → I say: you're compelled to turn up and be counted, not to *choose*; a blank/spoiled ballot is allowed, so no one's speech is overridden — only apathy loses its excuse.
