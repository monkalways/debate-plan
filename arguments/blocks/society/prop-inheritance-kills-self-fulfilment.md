ARGUMENT: inheritance kills self-fulfilment (unearned wealth removes the striving that gives life meaning)
Side: Prop        Motion type: Abolition
Burden it serves: that abolishing inheritance benefits the would-be heirs themselves — turns the "you're hurting the kids" intuition.

CLAIM: Children denied an inheritance are better off, because they're forced onto the effortful journey that produces genuine self-fulfilment.

CHAIN:
  L1  Abolish inheritance → heir has no guaranteed windfall
      Warrant: with the transfer banned, the recipient's living standard depends on their own output, not a bequest.
      Evidence: — (none brought; coach flagged zero evidence)
  L2  No windfall → heir must work, study, take on challenges
      Warrant: absent a fallback, the rational path to the wealth they want is to earn it themselves.
  L3  Earned effort → self-fulfilment
      Warrant: fulfilment comes from the journey and overcoming challenge, not from possessing money — the person builds "a story to tell."

IMPACT: heirs live meaningful, self-authored lives (pride, growth) instead of coasting — a lifetime effect, not a one-off. Value: individual flourishing.
  Weighing: stakeholder-priority / magnitude — a lifetime of fulfilment for the heir beats the short-term comfort of a windfall. (Asserted, not compared, as delivered.)
