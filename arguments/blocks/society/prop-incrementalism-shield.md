ARGUMENT: a partial solution isn't a failed one — the "step by step" shield
Side: Prop        Motion type: Policy (recurring rebuttal move — transfers to any "won't fully solve X" attack)
Burden it serves: defeats "this doesn't eliminate obesity, so it fails"

CLAIM: Reject the demand for a total solution; measure the policy against the status quo, not against perfection.

CHAIN:
  L1  Opp demands a total fix → sets a bar no single policy clears → any policy "fails" it
      Warrant: obesity is multi-causal (diet, exercise, income), so no one lever ends it — the standard is rigged.
  L2  A marginal reduction still prevents real harm → each deterred consumer is a genuine health gain
      Warrant: harm reduction is cumulative; deterring hundreds of thousands of marginal buyers is a real effect even if not everyone.
  L3  Therefore partial progress beats the counterfactual
      Warrant: doing nothing locks in 100% of the harm; a partial cut strictly dominates it.

IMPACT: policy is justified on marginal net benefit vs. status quo.
  Weighing: reversibility/counterfactual — compare to the status quo (which keeps the full harm), not to an imaginary total cure.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: taxing soda won't end obesity.  → I say: it doesn't need to — the metric is reduction, not elimination, and reduction is real.
  They say: obesity has many causes, one tax won't fix it.  → I say: multi-causal problems need multi-front action; removing one driver still helps, and this is one front.
