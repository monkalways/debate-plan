ARGUMENT: abolition frees the testator to enjoy their own money
Side: Prop        Motion type: Abolition
Burden it serves: benefits the wealth-holder (testator welfare), not only the heir.

CLAIM: Without the norm and pressure to bequeath, retirees spend on themselves and finally enjoy the life they saved for.

CHAIN:
  L1  Abolish inheritance → pressure to hoard for heirs disappears
      Warrant: the "greedy monster" norm and the felt duty to leave most of one's estate vanish once transfer is banned.
      Evidence: —
  L2  No pressure to hoard → retiree consumes their own savings
      Warrant: money previously earmarked for heirs is freed for personal use.
  L3  Consumption → testator welfare
      Warrant: spending on oneself delivers the fulfilment the person deferred across a whole working life.

IMPACT: retirees get the retirement they earned; less lifelong deferral and end-of-life regret. Value: testator welfare.
  Weighing: timeframe — realised enjoyment in one's own lifetime beats a deferred benefit passed to heirs. (Asserted, not compared.)

  They say: your own model still lets people gift / pay off mortgages "proportional to wealth" — so the pressure and hoarding survive.  → I say: tighten the model; as delivered the gifting carve-out guts the abolition and this chain with it.
