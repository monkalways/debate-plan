ARGUMENT: banning removes toxic upward social comparison
Side: Prop        Motion type: Policy
Burden it serves: second mechanism under the mental-health substantive

CLAIM: Banning social media protects mental health by removing the unattainable, fake social comparison platforms force on children.

CHAIN:
  L1  Curated/edited feeds → children compare upward
      Warrant: feeds surface idealised content (Photoshop/CapCut, "$60k/month as a teen"); kids caught off-guard can't discount it.
      Evidence: —
  L2  Upward comparison to fake standards → body dysmorphia, inadequacy, FOMO
      Warrant: the target is unachievable and fake, so the gap reads as personal failure.
      Evidence: —
  L3  Ban removes the feed → comparison stops → self-worth and time-on-self recover
      Warrant: no access = no exposure; children invest in personal development instead.

IMPACT: reduced body dysmorphia / inadequacy across youth.
  Weighing: shares the mental-health substantive's magnitude + irreversibility axis (folds under prop-break-dopamine-loops).

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: teach media literacy, don't ban.  → I say: kids are already taught not to trust it, but being caught off-guard by "your dream life" is hard to ignore — literacy doesn't beat an engineered feed.
  They say: comparison also motivates.  → I say: comparison to fake/edited standards is unachievable, so it demotivates and harms, not motivates.
