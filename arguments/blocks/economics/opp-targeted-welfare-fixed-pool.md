ARGUMENT: targeted welfare beats a universal payout from a fixed pool
Side: Opp        Motion type: Policy
Burden it serves: proves a better comparative counter-model exists — the same money helps the poor more if concentrated, so UBI isn't the best use of the fiat.

CLAIM: A fixed welfare budget aimed only at the poor helps them more than the same money spread across everyone.

CHAIN:
  L1  Universal, equal payment → most of the budget goes to the non-poor
      Warrant: the pool is fixed and the rich receive an equal share, so the majority who aren't poor absorb most of it.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#Canada poverty rate]] — only ~7–10% of a country is in poverty → most dollars "leak" to people who don't need them
  L2  Diluted pool → less reaches each needy person
      Warrant: spreading a fixed sum across everyone thins the per-person slice for the people who actually need it.
      Evidence: —
  L3  Same pool, targeted → the poor get more per person + wraparound services
      Warrant: welfare programs are designed to identify need and connect people to training/housing/food, not just cut a cheque.
      Evidence: —

IMPACT: the marginalized get materially more support per dollar — the people the state has a duty to protect.
  Weighing: stakeholder-priority — even if UBI's lower bureaucracy means marginally more money survives (even-if), concentrated aid still delivers more to the poor per dollar.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: targeted welfare misses eligible people and has its own bureaucracy (Yoodli follow-up).  → I say: means-testing imperfections cost a fraction of what's wasted paying the 90%+ who don't need it — fix the targeting, don't abandon it.
  They say: UBI's simplicity means more money actually reaches people.  → I say: even if marginally less after admin, ours is specialized and concentrated — best bang per buck for the needy.
