ARGUMENT: growth is survival — a government's first duty is its people, not nature
Side: Prop        Motion type: Comparative
Burden it serves: establishes stakeholder priority — human survival outranks environmental goods, so growth wins the central clash.

CLAIM: A developing government's first duty is keeping its people alive, and growth is what feeds and employs them, so it must come before environmental protection.

CHAIN:
  L1  Prioritise growth → jobs & household income
      Warrant: infrastructure and industry employ citizens and put money in pockets.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#Extreme poverty decline]]
  L2  Jobs & income → survival + escape from poverty
      Warrant: income buys food, shelter, healthcare; lifts people off subsistence.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#China's poverty reduction]] (~800M)
  L3  Survival secured → the state discharges its core duty
      Warrant: a government exists first to protect its people's lives, not ecosystems.

IMPACT: lives saved now vs environmental goods deferred.
  Weighing: stakeholder-priority — a starving child's death is irreversible and outranks a squirrel's; the human is the higher-priority stakeholder.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: environmental collapse will kill far more people later.  → I say: that's long-run, and growth buys the capacity to prevent it [see prop-growth-buys-enforcement-capacity]; a state that lets people starve now to hedge a future harm has already failed its primary duty.
  They say: it's a false choice, do both.  → I say: resources are finite and prioritisation is the whole motion — a dollar on one is a dollar off the other.
