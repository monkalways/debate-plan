ARGUMENT: growth buys the fiscal capacity to actually protect the environment
Side: Prop        Motion type: Comparative
Burden it serves: neutralises "you're abandoning nature" — growth is the precondition for real (not paper) protection.

CLAIM: A poor country must grow first because only a stronger economy generates the fiscal capacity to enforce environmental protection at all.

CHAIN:
  L1  Prioritise growth → larger tax base & incomes
      Warrant: industry, infrastructure and jobs enlarge the taxable economy and lift citizens above subsistence.
      Evidence: [[examples-bank#China's poverty reduction]]
  L2  Larger tax base → funded enforcement capacity
      Warrant: environmental law needs money — inspectors, rangers, monitoring, clean-tech; a broke state can fund none of it.
      Evidence: —
  L3  Enforcement capacity → environment actually protected
      Warrant: laws only bite when someone is paid to police them; capacity converts into conservation.

IMPACT: durable protection + development, vs unenforced paper bans.
  Weighing: timeframe — opp may win the short run (untouched forest) but prop wins the long run because capacity compounds and a ban a poor state can't enforce protects nothing.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: growth locks in irreversible damage before capacity arrives.  → I say: poverty is degrading the environment right now (desperation deforestation) and an unenforceable ban doesn't stop it — growth is the only path that ends the damage rather than ignoring it.
  They say: rich countries polluted their way up and never cleaned it.  → I say: clean-tech is now cheap so developing states can leapfrog [[examples-bank#Renewable cost collapse]], and only a funded state can enforce anything.
