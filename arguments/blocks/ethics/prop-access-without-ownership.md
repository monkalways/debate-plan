ARGUMENT: you don't need to POSSESS an artifact to give the world access — and "universal access" is really paywalled  🔁
Side: Prop        Motion type: Value/Policy   (rebuttal shield — rebuts the "encyclopaedic museum = access for all" line on any repatriation motion)
Burden it serves: defuses opp's biggest benefit ("only we let the whole world see it"), so ownership can return without the world losing access.

CLAIM: Access and ownership are separable — origin nations can share objects globally through loans, touring exhibitions, digital and replicas — and the "universal access" a big museum offers is really access for whoever can afford the money and the visa.

CHAIN:
  L1  Ownership returns to the origin nation → access does NOT have to fall
      Warrant: sharing runs on loans, touring shows, high-quality replicas and digitisation — none of which require the holder to keep title.
  L2  Meanwhile "see it all in London" access is gated
      Warrant: a person in the origin country needs airfare, a visa and time to reach a London or Paris gallery — so "belongs to all humanity" in practice means "available to the wealthy and mobile."
  L3  Return + sharing → wider real access than the status quo
      Warrant: the object can tour AND sit among the community it came from; more people, including the ones it means most to, actually reach it.

IMPACT: opp's "access for everyone" benefit is smaller than it looks and survives return anyway — so it can't outweigh the ownership wrong.
  Weighing: scope — count who really gets access, not who theoretically could; return widens the pool the status quo excludes.

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: encyclopaedic museums let millions see world cultures in one place.  → I say: millions who can afford London — loans and touring exhibitions bring the object to far more people, and digitisation reaches everyone the gallery never could.
  They say: origin countries will just hide it away.  → I say: they get to CHOOSE — and they can loan it back for safekeeping or a tour; ownership decides where it travels, it doesn't lock it in one room. [[prop-ownership-vs-custody]]
