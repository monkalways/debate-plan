ARGUMENT: rights track moral agency → animals owed welfare, not inviolability
Side: Opp        Motion type: Abolition (also Value)
Burden it serves: defeats Prop's strongest card ("animals have a RIGHT not to be used as tools") on its own terms, BEFORE any consequentialist weigh.

CLAIM: The animal right Prop invokes doesn't exist at full strength — animals are owed welfare (no needless suffering), not inviolability (never being used to help others).

CHAIN:
  L1  A right → grounded in moral agency
      Warrant: a right is one side of a reciprocal obligation; a rights-holder is one who can recognise and honour duties back.
      Evidence: —
  L2  Animals lack moral agency → can't enter the reciprocal contract
      Warrant: an animal can't hold or discharge obligations, so it can't claim the correlative protection humans grant one another.
      Evidence: —
  L3  No agency → owed welfare, not inviolability
      Warrant: sentience grounds a duty not to inflict needless suffering (welfare); it does NOT ground an absolute veto on ever being used (inviolability). Prop blurs the two.

IMPACT: Prop's rights case fails on its own terms — before we even reach the millions of human lives held in reserve.
  Weighing: stakeholder-priority / principle — a "right" that was never absolute can't outweigh a real one; you win the principle without needing the consequences. (Two-layer defense: principle first, consequences in reserve.)

THEY SAY → I SAY:
  They say: then infants / the severely disabled have no rights either (they lack agency).  → I say: rights attach by species-typical capacity and moral-community membership, not a case-by-case agency audit — we protect the human kind, not each person's faculties.
  They say: sentience alone grounds a right against being used.  → I say: sentience grounds welfare (don't torture), not inviolability (never use) — we honour the first, deny only the second.
