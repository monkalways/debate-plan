# Weekly Runbook — Practice, Grow the Bank, Consolidate

**Purpose:** the once-a-week work that the daily micro-sessions don't cover — a full graded
speech, growing the bank from it, cleaning up, and consolidating what you've learned so it
doesn't leak back out.

> The daily runbook builds recall. The weekly runbook does three things dailies can't:
> **produce new material** (the speech), **capture it** (`/distill-arguments`), and
> **lock in the old** (spaced review of what you've "mastered").

---

## Suggested week shape
| Day | Focus |
|---|---|
| Mon–Fri | The **daily runbook** — warm-up + a graded practice speech (+ Take 2 when there's a leak) |
| **Sat** | **Distill the week's speeches into the bank + hygiene** *(the big one)* |
| **Sun** | **Anti-pattern review + spaced-repetition sweep + set next week's focus** |

*(The full recorded speech now lives in the **daily** runbook — you do one most days. The weekly
job is to harvest that week's day-files into the bank and consolidate. Each item below happens
once a week.)*

---

## Saturday — capture the week

### ☐ 1. Run `/distill-arguments` on the week's day-files
- Point it at the `daily-records/day-N.md` speeches you produced this week. It mines them into new
  argument blocks (filed into the right `arguments/blocks/<theme>/` folder) and enriches
  `mechanism-bank.md` / `examples-bank.md` — web-verifying any new stat first.
- **Bonus:** you re-encounter your own material a week later — that spacing *is* revision.
- *(Optionally run it same-day after a standout speech, too — but batching the week is the default.)*

### ☐ 2. Bank hygiene (5 min)
- Skim the run's report: did new blocks land in the right topic folder? New mechanisms/examples
  in the right **section** (not a trailing batch)?
- Chase down any **⚠️ "held pending verification"** stat (currently: SQ Magazine 80/20;
  Przewalski's horse; AZA zoo attendance) — confirm the figure or leave it flagged. Never let an
  unverified number become a citable "fact."

---

## Sunday — consolidate & aim

### ☐ 3. Anti-pattern review (5–10 min) — arguably the highest-value 10 min of the week
- Open `arguments/blocks/README.md`, read every **⚠️** block and *why* it flopped
  (false "350×" stat · the "maybe we shouldn't use any animal" self-concession · the inheritance
  "caricature" · the Finland fabrication). Re-reading the failure is how you stop repeating it.

### ☐ 4. Spaced-repetition sweep (10 min)
- Re-test the items you marked **mastered** during the week — one cold rep each. Anything that
  slipped goes back into daily rotation. (Master → forget → re-master is normal; the sweep catches it.)

### ☐ 5. Set next week's focus (5 min)
- Open `plans/progress.md`, read the **open items** / top recurring fixes. Pick **one skill focus**
  for the week (e.g. current #1: kill the "maybe" hedging; or "change the seat" → try a
  3rd-speaker/reply role) and **one topic folder** to drill in the dailies.
- Jot both at the top of next week — it steers Steps 4 and the daily rotation.

---

## Monthly-ish (do when it applies, not every week)
- ☐ **Freshness pass:** any stat you lean on repeatedly — re-confirm it's current (judges punish a
  visibly out-of-date number). Round numbers over false precision.
- ☐ **Coverage check:** which motion *types* / *themes* have thin block coverage? Aim a practice
  speech there so the bank grows evenly (right now Politics & Society domains are only seeded).

## The weekly metric
Two questions: **(1)** did the bank grow this week (≥1 new block)? **(2)** is this week's
recurring-fix from `progress.md` actually shrinking take-over-take? If both are yes, you're
compounding.
