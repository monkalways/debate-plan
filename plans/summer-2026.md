## The two goals (his words)
1. **Speed:** Prepare a full **6-minute speech in 15 minutes** on an unseen motion.
2. **Library:** Build organized **storage of motion types, arguments, and mechanisms**.

> **Coach's reframe of Goal 1:** Speed is a *symptom* of preparation, not a skill you
> train directly. A debater preps fast when their mechanisms and examples are
> automatic — which is exactly what the Knowledge and Library tracks build. So we let
> the prep clock **fall out naturally** as the library grows. The clock is a
> **thermometer, not the furnace.** If hitting the clock ever forces him to drop
> weighing, the rep *failed* — slow down.

## Strategy — how this plan attacks his profile
Lucas is already a **confident speaker (7/10)** and **fast thinker (7/10)** — maintain
and exploit, don't rebuild. Effort loads onto the things that actually move ballots:

- **Weighing & clash (his real ceiling).** Coach's call: a confident, fast speaker
  with a deep library tends to give three beautiful points and then *talk past* the
  other team. Two debaters with identical material — the one who tells the judge *why
  their side outweighs* wins. This is now the #1 trained skill, and it's where his
  fast-thinking strength finally converts into points.
- **Knowledge (5/10)** → daily reading that **produces a library entry**, so
  knowledge-building and Goal 2 are the same hour of work.
- **Writing fluently & interestingly (5/10)** → a style track, drilled and reviewed on
  recordings.
- **Speed (Goal 1)** → an *output* of the above, monitored not chased.

## Weekly template (repeat each week; swap the domain & clock)
Each day = **Block A** (knowledge) + **Block B** (skill drill) + **Block C** (micro-drills).
**Sunday is a genuine off-day** — no logging, no reading, nothing. Recovery is part of the plan.

| Day | Block A — Knowledge (30 min) | Block B — Skill drill (60 min) | Block C — Micro-drills (20 min) |
|-----|------------------------------|--------------------------------|---------------------------------|
| **Mon** | Read domain source #1 → notes | **Speed case-write #1** (timed, *ends with protected weighing pass*) | 90-sec weighing drill + POI drill |
| **Tue** | Read domain source #2 → notes | **Live-clash / rebuttal drill** (see protocol) | POI drill + 2-min impromptu |
| **Wed** | Deep-dive: 1 issue in depth | **Build argument blocks + weakest-link tags** (→ library) | Signposting + 90-sec weighing |
| **Thu** | Read domain source #3 → notes | **Speed case-write #2** (timed + weighing pass) | Record; self-score with rubric |
| **Fri** | Review week's notes | **FDT coach session** — mock round, POIs, feedback | (in session) |
| **Sat** | (examples are captured during the week's reading — no separate task) | **Style rewrite** + **peer recording-swap** (each offers 2 POIs) | Record full 6-min; rubric review + quick library tidy |
| **Sun** | — **OFF** | — **OFF** | — **OFF** |

**Deload week (Week 4):** halve Block B, drop the second speed-write, no new knowledge
domain. Spend the freed time on **clash reps** and consolidating Weeks 1–3 into the
library. Purpose: prevent burnout and let skills settle. A plan with no permission to
plateau gets abandoned.

## Speed case-write protocol (Track 5)
Run Mon and Thu. Clock is the week's number, treated as a **thermometer**.

1. Pick an **unseen motion**.
2. Start the timer. Prep in this fixed sequence — *no backtracking*:
   - **Define + motion type** → pick the matching mechanism family from the bank.
   - **Draft 3 arguments** as 4-link chains (a→b→c, ¬a→¬b→¬c) — pull from the bank.
   - **1 example** per argument.
   - **PROTECTED final 90 sec — comparative weighing pass:** "Even if they win their
     point, we win because we outweigh on **scope / probability / magnitude /
     reversibility / timeframe**." *This block is sacred. If the clock forces you to
     cut it, the rep failed — slow the clock down next time.*
   - **Intro framing + signposted roadmap.**
3. Deliver the **6-min speech** (recorded).
4. Log: prep time, speech length, **and a separate weighing score**.

## Weighing & Clash (Track 1 — the priority)
The skills that win close rounds. Coach: *"At Worlds, the 3rd speaker who can't weigh
loses close rounds."*

- **Daily 90-sec weighing drill.** After any case, set a 90-sec timer and write ONLY
  the comparative — which side outweighs and on which metric, and why. Weighing is a
  muscle; isolate it daily.
- **Live-clash / rebuttal drill (Tue).** Not rebuttal against a stationary stored case —
  practice the live move: *"They said X → here's why X is wrong / doesn't matter /
  is true-but-irrelevant."* Use a fresh opponent argument each time (coach list, a
  peer, or an AI sparring partner). This is where his fast-thinking strength cashes in.
  **On solo days, ask an AI to fire one fresh opposing argument or POI at you** — otherwise
  this drill silently degrades into rehearsing a stationary case, which is the failure mode.
- **POI drill (daily, in Block C).** WSDC lives on points of information. He speaks;
  a timer/sibling/app prompts a POI mid-flow; he takes it and rebuts in one sentence
  *without losing his thread.* Also practise **giving** sharp POIs (2 per speech on the
  peer swap) — offering them is half the responsiveness score. Plays to his confidence.
- **Round-position drill (from Week 5).** Don't only train the constructive speech.
  Practise the **3rd-speaker** speech (rebuttal + weighing, *no new arguments*) and the
  **4-min reply** (a biased summary of why you won). This is his next real ceiling at Worlds.

## Measurement — rubric & log
Score every recorded speech 1–10 on six dimensions (mapped to his model of a good
debater; **weighing and clash are now their own lines, not buried**):

| Dimension | What it measures |
|-----------|------------------|
| **Knowledge & depth** | mechanisms, examples, nuance (weakness) |
| **Structure & logic** | signposting; do the 4-link chains hold? |
| **Weighing & comparative** | does he tell the judge *why his side outweighs*? (the ceiling) |
| **Clash & responsiveness** | rebuttal, engagement, POIs |
| **Style & fluency** | vivid, persuasive language (weakness) |
| **Speed & delivery** | prep within clock; fills 6 min; confidence (strength) |

**Tracking log** (`plans/progress-log.md`):

| Date | Motion | Prep | Length | Know | Struct | **Weigh** | Clash | Style | Speed | Note |
|------|--------|------|--------|------|--------|-----------|-------|-------|-------|------|

**Feedback surface (Coach's red flag — solo self-review can't catch substance errors):**
self-review on recordings is for *delivery and pace*; **weighing and clash must be
checked by another human** — the weekly FDT coach session + a weekly peer
recording-swap ("you watch mine, I watch yours"). Don't let five solo days groove in
the very habits we're fixing.

**Checkpoints:** Week 1 baseline → Week 3 mid-test → Week 6 pre-camp → Week 7 final.
