# Daily Runbook — Speech Practice + Knowledge Bank

**The daily loop:** review the bank → use it to prep a case fast → deliver & record → grade →
plug the holes in a Take 2 → log. The review *feeds* the speech; the speech *feeds* the bank
(weekly). That's the whole flywheel.

**Time:** ~45–60 min (10 warm-up + ~35–45 speech loop). On a rest day, do the warm-up alone.

> ### The two rules (live by them)
> 1. **Active recall, never re-reading** — cover the page, reconstruct out loud, then check.
> 2. **Scaffolds, not scripts** — rehearse the *chain + example*, never canned lines.

---

# Part A — Warm-up: bank recall (~10 min)
Primes retrieval so the material is *available* when the 15-min prep clock is running.

- ☐ **Type drill (3 min):** 8 motions from `motions/motion-list.md` → say **type + burden** each,
  under 90 sec (`motions/motion-taxonomy.md`).
- ☐ **Mechanism recall (4 min):** 1 chain from `arguments/mechanism-bank.md` — Aff + CF + **weakest
  link**, from memory, then check.
- ☐ **Example recall (3 min):** 2 entries from `arguments/examples-bank.md` — stat (rounded) +
  "use it for" + caveat.

*(Pick items by rotation: yesterday's misses → ⚠️ anti-patterns → never-recalled → newest blocks.
3–5 items/day, spaced. See `arguments/blocks/README.md`.)*

---

# Part B — The speech practice loop (the main event)

### ☐ 1. Set up the day file + today's focus (2 min)
- New `daily-records/day-N.md` with the **motion + side**. *(Ask me / the coach to prep a motion +
  session-focus brief if you want one loaded — like the top of `day-11.md`.)*
- Write **ONE focus** at the top, pulled from yesterday's misses or the #1 recurring fix in
  `plans/progress.md`. **Current #1: kill the "maybe" hedging** (concede once, on purpose).

### ☐ 2. Prep on the clock — 15 min  *(the speed-writing goal)*
Pre-flight order — **do it in this sequence, every time:**
1. ☐ Motion **type → burden** (30 sec).
2. ☐ **Write your 5-word button FIRST** — answer your crystallize question, then STOP
   (`arguments/templates/conclusion.md`). It goes on the sheet *before any argument*.
3. ☐ Name your **ONE weighing axis** (concede → axis → warrant → adjudicate; no 4-axis shotgun).
4. ☐ **Two mechanism-led arguments** — pull the **chain** from `mechanism-bank`, one **example**
   each from `examples-bank`, and a **🔁 rebuttal shield** for their best line (from `blocks/`).
   *This is what the bank is for — assemble, don't invent.*
5. ☐ If Opp: **engage their BEST line**, not the easiest. No double-billing.

### ☐ 3. Deliver & record — ~6 min (Yoodli)
- One take, full 6 minutes, recorded.

### ☐ 4. Capture (2 min)
- Paste the **transcript** + **Yoodli feedback** into the day file under `## Transcript` /
  `## Yoodli Feedback`.

### ☐ 5. Grade (content)
- Get the **debate-coach** content score + fix-by-fix. *(Ask me to run it.)*

### ☐ 6. Take 2 — plug the holes (~20 min)  *(when there's a clear leak)*
- Read the **top 2–3 fixes**. This is a **plug-the-hole redo, not a rebuild** — same motion.
- Write the redo focus at the top of a `# Take 2` section, re-prep only the leaks, re-record,
  re-capture, re-score. *(This two-take cycle is where most of your point gains have come from.)*

### ☐ 7. Close-out & log (3 min)
- Log the score(s) to `plans/progress.md`.
- **Last-8-seconds drill:** rehearse only the button a few reps — groove the *stop*.
- Note today's **misses + tomorrow's #1 fix** (feeds Part A rotation + tomorrow's focus line).

## Pre-speak discipline checklist (glance before every take)
The recurring fixes, distilled from your last 10 days — a 20-second scan:
- ☐ **Zero "maybe"** — concede once, on purpose *(current #1)*
- ☐ **Preview number = delivered number** (roadmap matches the conclusion)
- ☐ **No "But now Yoodli"** — there's a judge in the room, not the app
- ☐ **Mechanism over fake precision** — never fabricate a stat; round or reason instead
- ☐ **Land the button DRY** — no leading "So," nothing after your last 5 words
- ☐ **"refutation," not "reputation"**

## The metric
Not "did I practice" but: **is the content score trending up, and is today's targeted fix
actually shrinking take-over-take?** (e.g. Yoodli's "maybe" count dropping T1 → T2.)
