# Day 1 — Week 1 (Monday)

Day 1 is special: it's onboarding **+** your **baseline test**, so budget ~2 hours this
once. The normal Block A/B/C rhythm starts Tuesday. (Today, the baseline test *is* your
Block B.)

> **Goal of today:** not a polished speech — it's to set the honest starting number that
> everything else is measured against. Finding gaps today is the point.

---

## Before you start (5 min) — set up
- Open these in tabs: `START-HERE.md`, `motions/motion-taxonomy.md`,
  `motions/motion-list.md`, `plans/progress-log.md`.
- Get a **timer** (phone) and a **recording device** (video is best — it catches delivery).
- Have paper or a blank doc ready for prep notes.

---

## Step 1 — Learn the taxonomy (15 min)
- Read `motions/motion-taxonomy.md` top to bottom.
- **Focus on the "Quick identifier" table** — that's the one you'll use under the clock.
- Don't memorise. Just get the feel: *stem → type → what you must prove.*

## Step 2 — Type Drill, Set A (5 min)
- Open `motions/motion-list.md` → **Type Drill sets → Set A**.
- **Cover the answer key.** Read each of the 8 motions aloud; say its **type** + **the one
  thing you must prove**.
- Time it — aim for all 8 in **under 90 seconds**. Then check the key.
- Missed some? Glance back at the taxonomy. (It'll feel slow today — that's normal; this
  drill repeats daily.)

---

## Step 3 — The baseline speed test (50 min)
The most important part of Day 1. **Build it yourself** — you may use the banks (it's an
open-book skill), but write the arguments in your own words; don't copy a worked case.

**Your motion:** *THW tax sugary drinks* — argue **Proposition.** (🟢 Economics.)

### Prep — 30 minutes on the clock
Start the timer. Follow this order, **no backtracking**:

| Time | Do this |
|------|---------|
| **0–4 min** | Define terms ("tax" = significant levy on sugary soft drinks). Name the **type** (Policy → show net benefit vs. status quo). Set your **measure** (public health + who's helped). Decide your **3 argument labels**. |
| **4–16 min** | Write **3 arguments** as 4-link chains (a→b→c, ¬a→¬b→¬c). Reach into `arguments/mechanism-bank.md` → Economy: **Incentive Distortion** and **Market Failure / Externality** fit perfectly. Adapt them to soda — don't recite. |
| **16–24 min** | Add **one real example** per argument. `arguments/examples-bank.md` → **tobacco taxes** (price changes behaviour even for addictive goods) is ideal here. |
| **24–28 min** | **Protected weighing pass.** For each argument: *"and this outweighs because…"* (scope / magnitude / probability / reversibility / timeframe). **Do not skip this.** |
| **28–30 min** | Write your **intro** (pick a frame from `reference/framing-and-style.md` — the *characterisation* frame works well: a specific kid, a specific harm) + a **15-second roadmap**. |

### Deliver — 6 minutes
- Stop prep at 30:00 no matter what.
- **Record** the full speech. Aim to fill ~6 minutes.
- Don't restart if you stumble — a baseline should be honest.

---

## Step 4 — Score and log (15 min)
- Play the recording back once.
- `plans/progress-log.md` → **"Week 1 — Baseline"** box: fill motion + date.
- Score **1–10** on the six dimensions (be honest — a flattering baseline is useless):
  - **Know** — used a real example, not just assertion?
  - **Struct** — did the 4-link chains hold? Signposted?
  - **Weigh** — did you say *why you outweigh*?
  - **Clash** — n/a today (no opponent).
  - **Style** — vivid? Few fillers?
  - **Speed** — prep fit 30 min? Speech filled 6 min?
- Add a one-line row to the **Daily quick log** too.
- Write your **honest read on your weakest two dimensions** — that's your focus this week.

## Step 5 — Close (5 min)
- One sentence: *the single thing to fix tomorrow.*
- Tomorrow (Tue) is the **live-clash drill** — line up your AI sparring partner or a peer.

---

### What "good" looks like on Day 1
Don't expect polish — **expect to find the gaps.** A baseline that exposes weak weighing
or thin examples is doing its job. The number being *low* today is the whole point; the
plan exists to move it.
